## 1. Vision Statement

A 2D top-down open-world sim set in a fictional American suburb, September 1997.

You play a youth-unit (15–17) in a sunny, cheery compound that is quietly a compliance apparatus. Your inner life — authenticity, alienation, compliance standing, scrip, independence, self-awareness — is tracked in visible stats. Every day you choose: comply or resist. The system doesn't announce itself. The reeducation facility is just called "school." Your custodians are called "mom" and "dad." But the paperwork, the intercom, the signage, the shift reports — they tell a different story.

You start normalized. As your stats shift, the illusion begins to crack. There is no win condition. Time drifts forward forever. You decide what to do with what you're starting to see.

**Tone reference**: *Catcher in the Rye* × *Severance*. Surface 90s nostalgia, structural dread. Suburb phoniness radar cranked to the surface.

## 3. Terminology (Dual-Register)

The game runs on two vocabularies simultaneously. This is the core aesthetic.

| Surface (spoken / casual) | Institutional (paperwork, signage, official) |
|---|---|
| School | Reeducation Facility / Youth Training Center |
| Parents / Mom / Dad | Assigned Custodians / Household Handlers |
| Kid / teenager / student | Youth-unit / Ward / Subject |
| Home / house | Assigned Domicile / Habitation Unit |
| Family | Household Unit |
| Neighborhood | Sector / Compound Zone |
| Town / suburb | Compound / District |
| Money / allowance | Scrip / Compliance Credits |
| Report card / grades | Compliance Assessment |
| Chores | Assigned Duties |

**Rule**: NPCs in casual dialogue use the Surface vocabulary. Institutional channels (intercoms, mail, TV ads, forms, hall passes, school PA) use the Institutional vocabulary. The dissonance IS the horror.

## 5. Character Creation System

At game start the youth-unit rolls for each assignment variable. Each roll shows a result; player can **accept or re-roll up to 3 times** per variable. Final roll sticks if re-rolls are exhausted.

Framing: the game presents this as an official "Compliance Profile" being generated by the system, not as "pick your backstory."

### 5.1 Creation Variables (Roll Order)

| Variable | Institutional label | Options |
|---|---|---|
| Assigned custodians | "Custodial Structure" | Both / Maternal-only / Paternal-only / Secondary-Custodian-blended |
| Co-occupants | "Sibling Unit Count" | None / Elder (relocated) / Elder (co-occupant) / Younger (co-occupant) |
| Socioeconomic tier | "Resource Class" | Tier 4 / Tier 3 / Tier 2 / Tier 1-Declining |
| Household climate | "Domicile Ambient" | Detached-cordial / Hostile / Dysregulated-warm / Neglect-documented |
| Social placement | "Compound Standing" | TBD — see §11 Known Gaps |

Additional variables may be added; each new variable becomes another roll step.

### 5.2 Starting Stats (all 0–100, fully visible)

| Stat | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Authenticity | How honestly the youth-unit is behaving vs. performing compliance |
| Alienation | Distance from people around them; isolation level |
| Social capital | Visible compound standing / compliance score |
| Scrip (money) | Raw dollars (displayed as $ on surface, "Credits" on official forms) |
| Independence | Autonomy from custodians/authority |
| Self-awareness | Ability to notice and name what's happening internally |

**Design note**: Self-awareness is the "wake-up meter." Low self-awareness = the compound feels normal. Rising self-awareness = institutional language starts becoming visible (the HUD reveals dystopian vocabulary it was hiding before, signs shift, NPC phoniness becomes audible).

### 5.3 Creation → Stats Mapping

Each creation variable contributes to starting stat values and to **starting self-awareness** (how awake the youth-unit begins).

Design principle: harder starting configurations (Neglect-documented ambient, Tier-1-Declining class, Paternal-only or absent custodians) → higher starting self-awareness, lower social capital, harsher early game with earlier awakening. Easier configurations → lower self-awareness, more comfortable opening, but slower to see the system.

Exact formula TBD — see §11 Known Gaps.

## 6. Core Loop

**Minute-to-minute**: Stardew-like. Wake in domicile, look at the day, decide what to do, move through the world, interact with NPCs, watch clock advance.

**Day-to-day**: The *awakening lens* threads through every interaction. Low-self-awareness players experience a nostalgic 90s suburb. High-self-awareness players see the compliance apparatus. Most of the game is the gradient between those two states.

**Progression**: No end. No win. Open-ended sandbox. The protagonist keeps waking up (or doesn't). The years keep turning.

---

## 7. Time System

- **Calendar**: Real 1997 calendar. Actual days of the week. 365 days per year.
- **In-game day**: Stardew-style — clock advances with player action (movement, interaction, waiting). Not real-time.
- **Start date**: First scheduled Reeducation Facility day, September 1997 (exact date TBD — probably a Tuesday after Labor Day).
- **End date**: None. Y2K passes through. Nothing special required mechanically — but whatever official communications the compound issues around Jan 1 2000 will be unsettling by design.

---

## 8. Reeducation Facility ("School")

- **Surface framing**: Everyone on screen calls it "school." Signage, PA system, hall passes, report cards use the institutional terminology ("Youth Reeducation Facility," "Compliance Assessment," "Mandatory Attendance Record"). Low-self-awareness youth-units don't notice. High-self-awareness youth-units read the signs.
- **Participation**: Optional every day. Non-attendance is recorded on official files that can be retrieved.
- **Mechanical shape**:
  - **Attending** = +compliance (social capital), +scrip via small rewards, –authenticity, –independence
  - **Skipping** = +authenticity, +independence, +self-awareness (slowly), –social capital, risk of Custodian consequences
- **Interior detail**: TBD — at minimum, entering time-skips to 3pm. Whether classrooms / hallways / cafeteria are visible spaces is open (§11).

## 9. NPC System

### 9.1 Roster Strategy
**4 fully unique hero NPCs** + **variant population** (base archetypes recolored/accessorized, ~60% production savings).

The 4 unique archetypes, reread through the dystopian frame:

| Archetype | Surface read | Institutional read |
|---|---|---|
| Loyalist | The perfect student / good kid | The model subject, compliance 100 |
| Rival | Competitive peer | Another youth-unit gaming the compliance ladder |
| Burnout | Withdrawn / defiant peer | A youth-unit the system has already broken — cautionary tale or ally? |
| Authority | Teacher / principal / adult | Compliance officer / Reeducation Facility staff |

### 9.1.1 The 4 Unique NPCs

Palette references use the `Palette` resource names (see `palette.md` + `res://resources/palette.tres`). Bridge/UI colors appear on a sprite only at or above the awareness threshold noted.

#### Ashley Pritchard — "The Loyalist"
- **Institutional label**: Compliance Subject #0041
- **Age / read**: 16. Cheerful, punctual, volunteers as hall monitor, pins Reeducation Facility merit badges to her backpack. Uses the institutional vocabulary casually *without noticing*.
- **Palette**: acid_cream polo + warm_ochre skirt, sky_cool_highlight headband, deep_shadow outline. Merit-badge pins render as signal_purple — visible at awareness ≥ 26.
- **Dialogue register**: Institutional vocabulary in casual speech ("Did you turn in your Household Evaluation?"). Low-awareness reads this as pep; high-awareness reads this as the voice of the compound in a teen's body.
- **Core horror**: she is genuinely happy. She isn't performing — the compound has built her.
- **Relationship vector**: the surface-friend who keeps nudging the youth-unit toward compliance. Every act of resistance costs standing with her.

#### Tyler Brandt — "The Rival"
- **Institutional label**: Compliance Subject #0119
- **Age / read**: 17. Sharp-elbowed, tracks everyone's Compliance Assessment scores, trades answer keys. Plays the system because he's winning it.
- **Palette**: acid_cream polo (rumpled), warm_ochre chinos, sky_cool_highlight varsity jacket. Jacket patch has signal_purple stitching — visible at awareness ≥ 26, resolves into a compound insignia at ≥ 51.
- **Dialogue register**: Surface vocabulary in public, institutional vocabulary when alone with the player ("my Standing dropped two points this week — you don't want my Standing, trust me"). He sees the compound clearly and has opted in.
- **Core horror**: he knows. He chose to dominate rather than escape.
- **Relationship vector**: hostile ally of convenience. Will help the youth-unit if it raises his own Standing relative to someone else.

#### Jess Halloran — "The Burnout"
- **Institutional label**: Compliance Subject #0237 — **FLAGGED**
- **Age / read**: 17. Smokes behind the Reeducation Facility loading dock. Hoodie up, headphones with no music, three consecutive skipped Compliance Assessments on file. Custodians speak about her in lowered voices.
- **Palette**: deep_shadow hoodie, warm_ochre wash jeans, deep_shadow boots. Hand-drawn glitch_magenta mark on the inside of her wrist — visible only at awareness ≥ 76.
- **Dialogue register**: the *only* NPC who uses institutional vocabulary deliberately, with sarcasm as resistance ("You going to your reeducation today? They can flag me again"). Low-awareness players hear bitterness; high-awareness players hear the first honest voice in the compound.
- **Core horror**: the system already knows her file is flagged. She knows they know. She's decided it doesn't matter.
- **Relationship vector**: ally potential that unlocks slowly — meaningful dialogue only at awareness ≥ 50. She is the mirror that tells the youth-unit they are beginning to see.

#### Mr. Gary Caldwell — "The Authority"
- **Institutional label**: Compliance Officer / Grade 11 Assessor
- **Age / read**: mid-40s. The friendly teacher. Coaches JV track in his off-hours. Softens bad news with a smile. Says things like "I just want what's best for you kids."
- **Palette**: acid_cream short-sleeve dress shirt, warm_ochre tie, warm_ochre khakis, deep_shadow loafers and frames. Tie pattern resolves into repeating signal_purple compound insignia at awareness ≥ 51. At ≥ 76, his voice over the PA briefly distorts into a flat compliance-officer cadence.
- **Dialogue register**: dual-use sentences — every line parses two ways. "You'll thank me later, I promise." "We just want to make sure everyone has the tools to succeed." Never breaks the surface register out loud. His paperwork, hall-pass slips, and PA announcements use the full institutional vocabulary.
- **Core horror**: he is sincerely kind. The apparatus operates through him. He believes in it.
- **Relationship vector**: gatekeeper. Controls attendance flags, hall passes, referrals. Non-hostile in every moment, consequential in every record.

## 10. World & Map (Phase 1 Scope)

**Phase 1 slice**: Youth-unit's assigned domicile + yard + adjacent sector street.

- Domicile interior: rooms TBD (likely youth-unit bedroom, living area, kitchen, sanitation, custodian quarters)
- Yard: front and/or back, mailbox (compound postbox), driveway
- Adjacent street: ~1 block visible, a neighbor's yard edge, maybe a sidewalk

Later phases expand to full sector → compound → distant zones. No full-compound map committed yet.

**Minimap**: Always-on UI element. Reveals only explored tiles. Unexplored shown as fog. Gives peripheral sense of compound scope without shrinking viewport detail.
